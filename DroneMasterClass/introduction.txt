1) Definition
A drone (UAV) is an unmanned aerial vehicle that can fly autonomously or remotely, often using propellers, sensors, and onboard computers.

2) Why it matters
Expands possibilities in surveillance, delivery, agriculture, and defense.

Demonstrates integration of aerospace + AI/ML technologies.

Foundation for future transport systems like eVTOL air taxis.

3) Core concepts
Forces of flight: Lift, Weight, Thrust, Drag.

Axes of movement: Roll, Pitch, Yaw.

Drone configurations: Mono, Bi, Tri, Quad, Hexa, Octa copters.

Components: Propellers, motors, ESC, flight controller, frame, sensors, power system.

5) How-to / Steps
Understand the four forces acting on flight.

Choose a configuration (quad, hexa, etc.) based on application.

Select components: frame, motors, propellers, controller, power system.

Assemble drone and connect ESC + flight controller.

Calibrate sensors (GPS, IMU) and test stability.

Add AI/ML modules (vision, autonomy) if required.
